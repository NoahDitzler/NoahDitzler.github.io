About H. G. Wells

H. G. Wells was born September 21, 1866 and died August 13, 1946. He was an English writer who is most known for his works of science fiction. He wrote more than 50 novels and an abundance of short stories. Apart from his works of fiction, he also wrote on social commentary, polotics, history, and satire. His involvement in the community of futurists lead him to write and foresee the birth of tanks, space travel, and even the world wide web.
